      Separate pages?

      I've been thinking, perhaps if the arguments are well-developed enough, or if different typings arise, each community/country/city could have its own page (such as United States of America). Extensive functional analysis (such as on Rick's blog entry on socionics' integral type) would become much easier. This is why having the conference pages (not proper articles anyway) in the Wikisocion namespace is a good idea. Other alternatives: 1) The two could be separate sections of the same page — though that seems kludgy to me. 2) Prefix the integral type pages with "Type of" or something — though that would make the page names unnecessarily long. Unless anyone can give good reasons for these alternatives, I'll go ahead and make the changes (the New York City one can redirect to its current location for now). Thehotelambush 23:33, 20 July 2007 (BST)

      I agree, but I would recommend "United States (integral type)" as titles. --Admin 10:35, 21 July 2007 (BST)
