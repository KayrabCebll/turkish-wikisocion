      On 'Being Led around by (Fi)'

      Nice article, I could totally relate to a lot that was said here. These lines I found to be particularly true: "We will respond to you according to the rules of your reality". After seeing this, I kinda realized that I respond to people keeping in mind their 'reality' (or rather, what I perceive to be their reality) and trying to make sense of what I mean to convey in their own terms. That one statement I found particularly revealing. Also, this one: "But somehow, when we let others in and are therefore forced to see it through their eyes it looks so colorless and...ordinary...somehow." very, VERY true, I'd say. I now realize that's the reason I keep these kind of things to myself - too many subjective mental images associated with the ideas/experiences that makes it look awesome to oneself but lame/boring/crazy/all-of-the-above to others. And did I mention boring?

      Which brings me to the part which made me even want to come here to comment in the fist place: The last paragraph. I can totally, totally relate to the feeling. I mean, like every last bit of it. And while I can especially understand why you'd feel this is the 'most boring domain page' around, I feel this is the most genuine EII expression in this domain page (no offense to others who have contributed, of course). There are things like Overview of Model A or experiences with/views on other types, but these are rather 'generic things' and have become something of a 'standard feature' in domain pages by what I've seen. But this article is something only an EII could possibly end up writing . And believe it or not (heck, you're probably aware of this yourself), I found this 'boring talk' to be especially my kinda subject...it's probably that EII's are so used to having to look at things (and address them accordingly) from other people's point of view that they find it immensely relieving when someone is able to see things the way they do, for a change.

      Well, that's about it. I started out meaning to just speak my mind about what I liked in this article, and look how it ended up!! I tell you, expression is just not the thing for me. Too many thoughts occur to you at once before you can even collect them all, let alone put them into intelligible sentences. Next time I'll just 'digg', haha

      --ModestUser 08:38, 9 September 2010 (BST)

See my user page for further clarification)
